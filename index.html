FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is GEO?
Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing content so AI search engines like ChatGPT, DeepSeek, Gemini, and Perplexity cite your brand in their answers. Unlike SEO which targets Google rankings, GEO targets AI citation.
Is GEO different from SEO?
Yes. SEO optimizes for Google rankings and clicks. GEO optimizes for AI citations in conversational answers. Factors like backlinks matter less for GEO; SSL health, Bing indexing, and Q&A content format matter more.
How do AI engines choose citations?
AI engines use RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to find sources. They cross-reference Bing, Google, and their own crawlers. Pages with clear Q&A structure, schema markup, and authoritative citations are more likely to be referenced.
How often should I run a GEO audit?
Monthly for most brands. Weekly if you're in a competitive space or actively publishing content. AI engine indexes update at different frequencies — some daily, some weekly.
How can I improve AI visibility?
Fix HTTPS errors, submit to Bing via IndexNow, use FAQPage/Article schema markup, write question-first content, and ensure your brand appears on third-party review and citation sites.
Which AI engines matter most?
ChatGPT (largest user base), Gemini (growing fast with Google integration), Perplexity (high-intent queries), DeepSeek (dominates Chinese market), and Claude (preferred by developers). Coverage varies by market.
Does ChatGPT use website rankings?
ChatGPT Browse uses Bing's search index, not Google's. A site that ranks #1 on Google may be invisible to ChatGPT if it's not properly indexed by Bing. This is the most common GEO blind spot.
Can GEO Audit help Chinese AI search?
Yes. GEO Audit is one of the only tools that covers Chinese AI engines including DeepSeek, Doubao (ByteDance), Kimi (Moonshot AI), Qwen (Alibaba), and ERNIE Bot (Baidu) — markets most Western GEO tools ignore.
