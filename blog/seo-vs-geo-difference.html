What Is the Difference Between SEO and GEO?

Short answer: SEO optimizes for keyword rankings on Google and Bing to drive click-through traffic. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) optimizes for AI citation in ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and other LLM search tools. SEO gets you on the first page of results. GEO gets you inside the AI's answer.

If you've been doing SEO for years, GEO can feel like a completely different game. That's because it is. Generative Engine Optimization isn't "SEO for AI." It's a fundamentally different discipline with different targets, different tactics, and different success metrics.

Here's the exact breakdown.

SEO vs. GEO: The Core Difference

Dimension SEO GEO
Target Keyword rankings on Google SERPs AI citation in LLM responses
Goal Get clicks (traffic) Get cited (visibility in answers)
Content strategy Keyword density, backlinks, authority Direct answers, structured data, verifiable facts
Technical focus Meta tags, page speed, mobile-friendliness Schema.org JSON-LD, RAG-friendliness, chunkability
Off-page Backlinks, domain authority Cross-platform citations, brand mentions on forums/media
Success metric Traffic, bounce rate, conversions AI citation rate, brand mention sentiment, brand search lift
Traffic model User clicks → visits your site AI cites your brand → user searches for you directly

Why This Matters Right Now

Google's AI Overviews already appear for 25%+ of search queries. ChatGPT processes 400M+ weekly active users. Perplexity handles 100M+ queries per week. These are zero-click environments — users get answers without visiting any website.

If your brand isn't cited inside those AI-generated answers, you're invisible to a fast-growing share of your potential customers.

Can You Do SEO and GEO Together?

Yes — and you should. They're complementary, not competing. Many GEO-friendly practices (clear content structure, schema markup, authoritative sources) also help traditional SEO. The key difference is mindset:

  • Think SEO when you want to capture users who search on Google and click through to your site.
  • Think GEO when you want AI models to recognize your brand as the authoritative answer source for your niche.

The brands that win in 2026-2027 are the ones that optimize for both simultaneously.

Where to Start

GEO doesn't require a complete content overhaul. Start with one question your customers ask most often, create a single page that answers it directly with data and structure, and check if AI engines cite it. That's the GEO flywheel.
