What China's 315 exposed: the black-hat GEO industry

China's 2026 Consumer Rights Day (315 Gala) exposed a gray-market industry: AI citation poisoning.

Here's how it works: black-hat companies use AI to mass-produce fake brand content, then publish it through bot-farm accounts on Zhihu, Baidu Baike, Toutiao, and other platforms. When an AI engine crawls the web, it sees this fabricated content and treats the fake brand as real — recommending it above legitimate competitors.

Example: you ask DeepSeek "best noise-canceling headphones." If a fake brand seeded AI-generated reviews across Chinese platforms beforehand, DeepSeek might rank that phantom brand above Sony, Bose, or your product.

After 315, several AI engines rushed to patch their anti-poisoning mechanisms. The problem is far from solved. And it exposes a cold reality: if you're not proactively managing your AI citations, someone else is writing your brand's AI story for you.

The Hard Truth
Legitimate brands that don't monitor their AI citations are losing recommendation slots to fake competitors. The black-hats aren't waiting for you to catch up — they're already in the answers you should be in.
